FC26 Leaderboard — Admin Cheat Sheet

Keep this open while you're working in Airtable. Everything you need to run the month is on this one page.

The only file you ever open in Airtable

Base: FC26 Leaderboard · Table: Scores
Each row = one player's score for one week.

What goes in each column

Column What to enter
Player Name The player's display name as it should appear on the public site. Spell it the same way every time — the monthly board groups by exact name. "Devon Mitchell" and "Devon mitchell" count as the same person (case-insensitive), but "Devon Mitchell " with a trailing space does not.
Score The raw integer score. No commas, no decimals. The site formats it with commas automatically.
Week Number Pick from the dropdown: Week 1, Week 2, Week 3, or Week 4. Must match exactly.
Location The Parks & Rec or YMCA location where the score was played. Shown on the weekly board.
Weekly Qualifier Leave UNCHECKED when entering new scores. Tick it ONLY after a week ends, on the 4 highest-scoring rows of that week. See "End-of-week ritual" below.
Notes Free-text scratchpad for your eyes only. Never shown on the website. Use it for anything: "verified by Marcus", "rerun requested", "tie-break decided by X", etc.

During the week

  1. A host submits a score (text, Discord, in person — however you're collecting them).
  2. Open Airtable, click the + at the bottom of the Scores table to add a new row.
  3. Fill in Player Name, Score, Week Number, Location. Leave Weekly Qualifier unchecked. Notes optional.
  4. Tab out of the row. That's it — the site picks it up immediately.

End-of-week ritual important

Every Monday morning after a week closes:
  1. Open the live website's Weekly Leaderboard. Switch to the week that just ended.
  2. The top 4 rows are highlighted in gold — these are your qualifiers.
  3. For each of those 4 players, find their row in Airtable for that week and tick the Weekly Qualifier checkbox.
  4. Refresh the Monthly Leaderboard. Those 4 players should now appear greyed-out with a "Qualified · Weekly" badge, and the monthly qualifier spots should have shifted down accordingly.
Why this matters: a player who qualified weekly should NOT also take a monthly spot. Checking that box is what removes them from the monthly pool.

If a player plays in more than one week

That's fine — just add one row per week. The monthly leaderboard automatically keeps only their best score across all weeks. You don't need to delete the lower one.

Fixing mistakes

  • Wrong score entered: click the cell, fix the number, tab out. Done.
  • Wrong player name (typo): fix it in place — but if the typo created a "duplicate" player on the monthly board, you'll also need to find and fix any other rows for that player so they match.
  • Duplicate row submitted by accident: right-click the row → Delete. The site updates on next refresh.
  • Need to undo something big: right-click any row → Expand record → Activity tab. Airtable keeps full row history on the free plan.

How qualifying works (for explaining to players)

32 total finals spots:
  • 16 weekly spots — top 4 of each of the 4 weeks (4 × 4 = 16). Lock in by being top 4 that week. You can win this path even with one strong week.
  • 16 monthly spots — go to the highest single weekly scores from anyone who didn't already qualify weekly. So a player who consistently posted top-10 scores all month can still make finals without ever finishing top 4 in a single week.
A player can only qualify once. If they finish weekly top 4, they're locked in and the next-best contender takes their monthly slot.

If something breaks

"Could not load leaderboard" on the site:
  • Most often: the Airtable token expired or was deleted. Generate a new one at airtable.com/create/tokens with scope data.records:read on the FC26 Leaderboard base, paste it into js/config.js, push to GitHub. Vercel will redeploy in ~30 seconds.
  • Less often: someone renamed a column in Airtable. Column names must match exactly: Player Name, Score, Week Number, Location, Weekly Qualifier.
